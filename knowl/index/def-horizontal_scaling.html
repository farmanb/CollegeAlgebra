Definition 2.3.13. Horizontal Scaling.

Let \(c > 1\) be a number. We say the function \(g(x)\) is a horizontal scaling of the function \(f(x)\) if \(g(x) = f(cx)\) or \(g(x) = f\left(\frac{1}{c}x\right)\text{.}\)
  • The graph of \(g(x) = f(cx)\) is obtained by compressing the graph of \(y = f(x)\) horizontally by a factor of \(c\text{.}\)
  • The graph of \(g(x) = f\left(\frac{1}{c}x\right)\) is obtained by stretching the graph of \(y = f(x)\) horizontally by a factor of \(c\text{.}\)
