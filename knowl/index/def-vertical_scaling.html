Definition 2.3.7. Vertical Scaling.
Let \(c > 1\) be a number. We say the function \(g(x)\) is a vertical scaling of the function \(f(x)\) if \(g(x) = cf(x)\) or \(g = \frac{1}{c}f(x)\text{.}\)
  • The graph of \(g(x) = cf(x)\) is obtained by stretching the graph of \(y = f(x)\) vertically by a factor of \(c\text{.}\)
  • The graph of \(g(x) = \frac{1}{c}f(x)\) is obtained by compressing the graph of \(y = f(x)\) vertically by a factor of \(c\text{.}\)
