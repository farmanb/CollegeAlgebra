Points in the Plane.

Definition 1.1.1.

The Cartesian plane is a grid system used to locate objects using two perpendicular directions. The horizontal direction is called the \(x\)-axis and the vertical direction is called the \(y\)-axis The plane is partitioned into four pieces called quadrants, depicted in Figure 1.1.2
The type of objects we are interested in locating will vary by application. In general, we will refer to these simply as points. A point is located in the plane by its position relative to the \(x\)-axis, called the \(x\)-coordinate and relative to the \(y\)-axis, called the \(y\)-coordinate.
By convention, we locate a point by first giving the \(x\)-coordinate, then the \(y\)-coordinate.

Definition 1.1.4.

An ordered pair, \((x,y)\text{,}\) is the data of two numbers where the order the numbers appear matters.
In Figure 1.1.3, the point \(P\) is given by the ordered pair \((3,2)\) and the point \(Q\) is given by the ordered pair \((2,3)\)
