Definition 5.6.1. Piecewise Defined Function.

Let \(n\) be a positive integer. Assume \(A_1, A_2, \ldots, A_n\) are pairwise disjoint sets, meaning whenever \(i \neq j \in \{1, 2, \ldots, n\}\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} A_i \cap A_j = \emptyset, \end{equation*}
and let \(A = A_1 \cup A_2 \cup \cdots \cup A_n\text{.}\) Suppose
\begin{equation*} f_1 \colon A_1 \to \R,\,f_2 \colon A_2 \to \R,\, \ldots,\, f_n \colon A_n \to \R \end{equation*}
are functions. The function \(f \colon A \to \R\) defined by
\begin{equation*} f(x) = \begin{cases} f_1(x) \amp \text{if}\ x \in A_1,\\ f_2(x) \amp \text{if}\ x \in A_2,\\ \amp\vdots\\ f_n(x) \amp \text{if}\ x \in A_n \end{cases} \end{equation*}
is called a piecewise defined function.
