Definition 2.4.10. Reflection of the \(x\)-axis.
We say the function \(g(x)\) is a reflection of \(f(x)\) if either \(g(x) = -f(x)\) or \(g(x) = f(-x).\)
  • The graph of \(g(x) = -f(x)\) is obtained by reflecting the graph of \(y = f(x)\) over the \(x\)-axis.
  • The graph of \(g(x) = f(-x)\) is obtained by reflecting the graph of \(y = f(x)\) over the \(y\)-axis.
