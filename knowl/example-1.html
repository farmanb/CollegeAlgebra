Example 1.2.2.

Consider the equation \(y = 2x + 3\text{.}\) The point \((1,5)\) is on the graph of equation because the equation
\begin{equation*} 5 = 2(1) + 3 = 2 + 3 \end{equation*}
is true.
The point \((3,3)\) is not on the graph of the equation because
\begin{equation*} 3 = 2(3) + 3 = 9 \end{equation*}
is not true.
More generally, the graph of the equation \(y = 2x + 3\) consists of all points of the form \((x, 2x + 3)\text{.}\)
