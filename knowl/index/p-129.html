A polynomial function of degree \(n\) is a function of the form
\begin{equation*} f(x) = a_nx^n + a_{n-1}x^{n-1} + \cdots + a_1x + a_0, \end{equation*}
where each of \(a_0, a_1, \ldots, a_n\) is a number called the coefficient of \(1, x, \ldots, x^n\text{,}\) respectively, and \(a_n \neq 0\text{.}\) In this section, we study a special kind of polynomial known as a linear function. As we will see, the name linear comes from the fact that the graph of such a function is always a line.
