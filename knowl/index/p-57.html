The type of things we are interested in locating will vary by application. In general, we will refer to these simply as points. A point is located in the plane by its position relative to the \(x\)-axis, called the \(x\)-coordinate, and relative to the \(y\)-axis, called the \(y\)-coordinate. By convention, we locate a point by first giving the \(x\)-coordinate, then the \(y\)-coordinate.
