Example 3.2.4.

Find the slope of the line through the points \((3,5)\) and \((6,2)\text{.}\)
Solution.
The slope of the line through the points \((3,5)\) and \((6,2)\) is
\begin{equation*} \frac{2 - 5}{6 - 3} = \frac{-3}{3} = -1. \end{equation*}
This says when the \(x\)-coordinate increases by 1, the \(y\)-coordinate decreases by 1.
