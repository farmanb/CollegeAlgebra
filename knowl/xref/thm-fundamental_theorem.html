Theorem 3.1.5 (Carl Friedrich Gauss). The Fundamental Theorem of Algebra.

Given a polynomial \(f\) of degree \(n > 0\text{,}\) there exist a constant \(c\) and \(n\) roots \(r_1, r_2, \ldots, r_n\) such that
\begin{equation*} f(x) = c(x - r_1)(x - r_2) \cdots (x - r_n). \end{equation*}
We call this expression the factorization of the polynomial \(f\text{.}\)
