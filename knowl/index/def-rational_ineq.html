Definition 5.1.7. Rational Inequality.

Assume \(p \colon \R \to \R\) and \(q \colon \R \to \R\) are polynomial functions. A rational inequality is a relation of the form
  • \(\frac{p(x)}{q(x)} \lt 0\text{,}\)
  • \(\frac{p(x)}{q(x)} \leq 0\text{,}\)
  • \(\frac{p(x)}{q(x)} > 0\text{,}\) or
  • \(\frac{p(x)}{q(x)} \geq 0\text{.}\)
