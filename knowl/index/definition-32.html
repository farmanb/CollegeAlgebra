Definition 3.3.1.

The slope of the line through the points \((x_1, y_1)\) and \((x_2, y_2)\) is
\begin{equation*} \frac{y_1 - y_2}{x_1 - x_2} = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}, \end{equation*}
provided that \(x_1 \neq x_1\text{.}\) The slope measures the rate of change in \(y\) with respect to \(x\text{,}\) as depicted in FigureÂ 3.3.2.
The slope of a line is only defined for lines that are not vertical. We say the slope of a vertical line is undefined.
