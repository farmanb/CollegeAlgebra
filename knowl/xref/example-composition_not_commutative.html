Example 5.3.6.

Consider the functions \(f \colon \R \to \R\) defined by \(f(x) = x + 2\) and \(g \colon \R \to \R\) defined by \(g(x) = x^2 + 3\text{.}\) Find the composition of \(f\) with \(g\text{.}\) Verify that it is not the same as the composition of \(g\) with \(f\text{.}\)
Solution.
The composition of \(f\) with \(g\) is the function \(f \circ g \colon \R \to \R\) defined by
\begin{align*} (f \circ g)(x) = f\left(f(x)\right) \amp= g\left(x^2 + 3\right)\\ \amp= \left(x^2 + 3\right) + 2\\ \amp= x^2 + 5\\ \amp\neq x^2 + 4x + 7 = (g \circ f)(x). \end{align*}
