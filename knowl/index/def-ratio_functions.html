Definition 4.1.11. Ratio of Functions.

Assume \(f \colon A \to \R\) and \(g \colon B \to \R\) are functions, and for every \(x \in B\text{,}\) \(g(x) \neq 0\text{.}\) The ratio of \(f\) and \(g\) is the function \(f / g \colon A \cap B \to \R\) defined by
\begin{equation*} \left(\frac{f}{g}\right)(x) = \frac{f(x)}{g(x)}. \end{equation*}
