The story is similar for multiplication. For any non-zero real number \(c\text{,}\) the multiplicative inverse of \(c\) is the real number \(1/c\) that satisfies
\begin{equation*} c \times \frac{1}{c} = 1\quad\text{and}\quad \frac{1}{c} \times c = 1 \end{equation*}
Multiplication by \(c\) and division by \(c\) define functions \(\mul{c}, \Div{c} \colon \R \to \R\) by
\begin{equation*} \mul{c}(x) = cx \quad\text{and}\quad \Div{c}(x) = \frac{x}{c} \end{equation*}
